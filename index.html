Metro to Nørreport, 5-min walk. One of Denmark's finest Renaissance castles — home to the Crown Jewels, royal regalia, and 400 years of royal treasures. Bus 6A from Central Station (or short Uber from Torvehallerne) — drops right at the main entrance. Address: Roskildevej 32, Frederiksberg. One of Europe's oldest and best zoos with 4,000+ animals. 🎓 YOUR CLASSES — Data Vis + DDM (12:00–16:00)
She has 4 hours — plenty for a solo afternoon. Suggested itinerary for her:

Option A — Botanical Gardens + Latin Quarter:
• Copenhagen Botanical Gardens (Øster Farimagsgade 2B, Nørreport) — Free entry to the outdoor gardens. The Victorian Palm House is a stunning cast-iron greenhouse dating to 1874 — one of the most beautiful spaces in Copenhagen. Very peaceful on a weekday afternoon. Budget 1–1.5h.
• The Latin Quarter — The old university neighbourhood surrounding the gardens. Wander along Fiolstræde and Studiestræde — lined with second-hand bookshops, independent cafes, and small boutiques. Option B — Nørrebro Vintage Shopping:
• Ravnsborggade & Elmegade — the best vintage and thrift strip in Copenhagen. Hit Episode (Ravnsborggade 11), UFF (Ravnsborggade 9), and Wasteland for quality second-hand finds. Then pop into the indie boutiques along Elmegade. Very local, very her.
• Jægersborggade (10 min walk) — Copenhagen's coolest small street: ceramics studios, zero-waste shops, indie art galleries and The Coffee Collective (no. 10) for the best coffee in the city. Perfect way to end the afternoon.

Option C — Museum afternoon:
• SMK — National Gallery of Denmark (Sølvgade 48–50) — Free permanent collection, impressive building, excellent cafe inside. 10 min walk from Nørreport. 2h is comfortable for the highlights. Send her Option A or B — both are self-explanatory and easy by metro

🎓 YOUR CLASSES — Ethical Hacking (10:00–14:00)
She has a solid 4-hour morning/early afternoon window. Suggested itinerary for her:

Option A — Vesterbro: Værnedamsvej + Istedgade:
• Værnedamsvej — Copenhagen's most charming street, nicknamed "Little Paris." A short stretch lined with French-style wine bars, artisan cheese shops, florists, and independent boutiques. Start with coffee at Riccos Kaffebar (Istedgade 119) — one of CPH's oldest and most beloved independent coffee spots.
• Istedgade — Vesterbro's main artery, now a genuinely cool strip of vintage stores, concept shops, and cafés. Den Blå Hest (Istedgade 66) is a great independent boutique. Samarkand (Istedgade 87) for quality second-hand. Completely different crowd and vibe from Nørrebro.

Option B — Museum + design:
• Designmuseum Danmark (Bredgade 68) — World-class design museum in a beautiful 18th-century building. Danish furniture, fashion, and industrial design. €14 entry, ~1.5–2h. Metro: Østerport, then 12 min walk.
• Bredgade antique street — The street the museum sits on is lined with antique and art galleries — great for window shopping after.

Option C — Assistens Cemetery + Jægersborggade:
• Assistens Kirkegård (Kapelvej 4) — One of Copenhagen's most beloved local spots. A vast, park-like cemetery where Hans Christian Andersen and Søren Kierkegaard are buried — locals picnic and sunbathe here. Beautiful tree-lined paths, completely off the tourist radar. Free, open all day.
• Jægersborggade — 10 min walk from the cemetery. Nørrebro's best-kept secret: The Coffee Collective (no. 10), indie ceramics studios, zero-waste shops, and small galleries.

Option D — Designer Quarter Shopping:
• Kronprinsensgade — Copenhagen's boutique street. Acne Studios (no. 11), Stine Goya (Grønnegade 11), Cecilie Bahnsen, Weekday (no. 6). Very different energy from the vintage shopping — this is Scandi minimalism at its finest.
• Strøget — the main shopping street for high street brands if she wants something more accessible. Connects back to Kronprinsensgade easily. Option A (Vesterbro) or D (designer quarter) best if she hasn't done vintage yet — save vintage for a fresh day
14:30

🏛 Roma

🇫🇷 Paris
🥐Best pastries: Du Pain et des Idées (boulangerie), Pierre Hermé (macarons), Stohrer (oldest patisserie), Ladurée (classic). All within easy reach of your flat!
🎭Crazy Horse Paris (Apr 11, 23:45): Booked ✓. Arrive by 23:15. Smart dress required. Address: 12 Avenue George V, 8th arr. Late finish — Day 13 starts at 10:30 AM.
🛍Shopping: Galeries Lafayette (5 min from flat — Art Nouveau dome + food hall), Le Marais vintage/boutiques, SoPi / Rue des Martyrs (Tue Apr 14), Porte de Vanves flea market (Sat/Sun mornings).
⛪Notre-Dame: Fully restored and reopened Dec 2024 — seeing the interior now is genuinely special. Free entry but timed slots are required and fill up weeks ahead. Book your 16:45 slot on Apr 14 at notredamedeparis.fr urgently.
🏰Disneyland tips: Tickets booked ✓. Captain Jack's at 19:15 ✓. Get there for park opening (9:00). Download the Disneyland Paris app for real-time wait times and Premier Access ride booking. Head to Big Thunder Mountain first.
🏰Versailles (Apr 11): RER C from Invalides or Musée d'Orsay (~35 min). Book "Passeport" at chateauversailles.fr — includes Palace, Gardens, and Trianon domain. Palace entry at 10:00. Lunch at La Flottille (book at laflottille.fr). Grandes Eaux Musicales on Saturdays — fountains to baroque music, check day's schedule. Eiffel Tower is on Tuesday Apr 14, not this day.
🚇Transport: RER B direct from CDG to Gare du Nord (~45 min, ~€11.80). Buy a carnet of 10 Metro tickets (€17.35) or use contactless card for getting around the city.
