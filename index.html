Palaces, Markets & Nørrebro
Copenhagen ▾
09:00
🥐 Breakfast — Andersen & Maillard (Nørrebro)
A cult Nørrebro bakery by two former Noma bakers — excellent pastries and a proper Nordic breakfast spread. The branch on Elmegade 15 is perfectly placed for the day ahead. Try the pistachio croissant or the rye roll with butter.
Hart Bageri saved for tomorrow — don't double up
10:00
📸 Church of Our Saviour — Spiral Tower
Metro to Christianshavn (M1/M2). The external spiral staircase winds around the outside of the 90-metre spire — one of the best views over Copenhagen. 400 steps total, 150 on the outside. Tower open 10:00–16:00 in April. ~€7pp. Address: Sankt Annæ Gade 29.
⚠ Book your slot in advance at vorfrelserskirke.dk — can reach capacity on busy Easter days.
📸 Go early before the queues build — stunning photography spot
12:00
🍗 Poulette — Lunch
Metro to Nørrebro — 10 min. Copenhagen's cult fried chicken sandwich on Møllegade 1 — Nashville-style chicken or mapo tofu on a brioche bun, pickled cucumber, mayo. Spicy by default — ask for mild if needed. Fries with Sichuan pepper mayo are essential.
⚠ Open Wed–Sun, opens at 12:00, until sold out — go straight from the church to guarantee a sandwich.
Hidden gem — one of CPH's most loved lunch spots
13:00
🏰 Rosenborg Castle & King's Garden
Metro to Nørreport, 5-min walk. One of Denmark's finest Renaissance castles — home to the Crown Jewels, royal regalia, and 400 years of royal treasures. Take your time in every room, especially the treasury in the basement. The castle closes at 17:00 in early April — you have 4 full hours, which is exactly what it deserves. Then as the castle closes, spill out into the King's Garden for a final stroll in spring bloom before heading to dinner.
Crown Jewels are spectacular — this is a proper afternoon attraction
Buy tickets at rosenborgslot.dk to skip the queue
19:00
🍽 Reffen Street Food Market
Copenhagen's coolest outdoor street food market on the waterfront of Refshaleøen. Dozens of vendors, incredible variety, young crowd, harbour views. Bus 9A from Nørreport or short Uber. Open until 22:00.
Hidden gem — most tourists don't make it here

The only building in the world housing all three branches of government — and home to Denmark's most spectacular palace rooms. Royal Reception Rooms, medieval ruins beneath the palace, Royal Kitchen and Royal Stables all included (~€26pp combined). Then climb the Christiansborg Tower for free — at 106 metres it's the tallest point in Copenhagen with 360° views over the city. Open from 10:00. Budget 1.5–2h total.

Proper lunch here — not just snacks

🦁 Copenhagen Zoo
Bus 6A from Central Station (or short Uber from Torvehallerne) — drops right at the main entrance. Address: Roskildevej 32, Frederiksberg. One of Europe's oldest and best zoos with 4,000+ animals. Highlights: pandas (Mao Sun and He Xing — very few zoos in Europe have them), polar bears in the Arctic Ring (underwater viewing tunnel), elephant house designed by Norman Foster, and the savannah with giraffes, zebras and rhinos. Budget 3–4 hours — it's 11 hectares and genuinely excellent. Closes at ~17:00–18:00 in April. ~€23pp.
Pandas + Norman Foster elephant house — genuinely one of the best zoos in Europe

Walk the Passeggiata degli Innamorati (Lovers' Walk) along the water, explore the cobblestone lanes, and visit Villa Monastero (€8pp) — one of the most stunning lakeside gardens in Italy, in full wisteria bloom in April.

~20 min ferry crossing across the lake — one of the great short journeys in Europe. Buy a free circulation ticket (~€23pp) at the ferry dock in Varenna which lets you hop on and off all day. Book ahead at navigazionelaghi.it — ferries sell out on Easter Saturday.
⚠ Buy ferry tickets in advance — Easter weekend = very busy

🌸 Bellagio — lunch + afternoon
The jewel of Lake Como — often called one of the most beautiful towns in Europe. Flower-adorned balconies, cobblestone streets, lakeside promenades and stairways with mountain views. Walk up Salita Serbelloni for panoramic views, browse the boutiques on Via Garibaldi, and wander down to the harbour.
Lunch rec: Ristorante Silvio or any lakefront restaurant — fresh perch from the lake (pesce persico) is the local specialty. Budget €25–35pp for a proper sit-down lunch with a view.
📸 Most photogenic town on the lake — take your time here

📸 Funicular to Brunate — the view from the top is breathtaking

Hidden gem — most tourists never come here

📸 Trastevere — afternoon wander
Rome's most photogenic neighbourhood. Ivy-covered walls, narrow cobbled lanes, flower-filled balconies — beautiful at every turn. Get gelato at Fatamorgana (Via Roma Libera 11 — creative flavours, one of Rome's best). Very lively on Easter Sunday afternoon.
📸 Best street photography neighbourhood in Rome
16:30
📸 Gianicolo Hill — Sunset viewpoint
The best panoramic view in all of Rome — better than any rooftop bar and completely free. The entire city spreads out below, St Peter's dome dominating the skyline. At sunset this is genuinely breathtaking. ~15 min Uber from Trastevere or a steep 20 min walk up.
Best photography spot in Rome — arrive 30 min before sunset
18:00
🏛 Pantheon
Head down from Gianicolo into the old city — Uber to Piazza della Rotonda (~10 min). One of the best-preserved ancient buildings in the world, still awe-inspiring after 2,000 years. The unreinforced concrete dome remains the largest in the world. ~30–40 min inside. ~€5pp. Open until 19:00 — arrive by 18:00 for a comfortable visit.
⚠ Book in advance at pantheonroma.com — timed entry required
Easter Monday may have limited hours — confirm before visiting
19:00
⛲ Trevi Fountain
A 10-min walk from the Pantheon. The most famous fountain in the world — 26 metres high, Baroque masterpiece, throw a coin over your left shoulder with your right hand to guarantee a return to Rome. Go in the evening when the crowds thin slightly and the floodlights make it genuinely beautiful. Free, no booking needed.
📸 Best photographed at night — the lighting is magical
19:45
🍹 Drinks — Campo de' Fiori → Piazza Navona
Campo de' Fiori has a lively young bar scene at night — outdoor tables, very relaxed. Then a 5-min walk to Piazza Navona for Bernini's famous fountain lit up at night — one of the best evening spots in Rome.
📸 Piazza Navona at night is stunning for photos

🎓 YOUR CLASSES — Data Vis + DDM (12:00–16:00)
She has 4 hours — plenty for a solo afternoon. Suggested itinerary for her:

Option A — Botanical Gardens + Latin Quarter:
• Copenhagen Botanical Gardens (Øster Farimagsgade 2B, Nørreport) — Free entry to the outdoor gardens. The Victorian Palm House is a stunning cast-iron greenhouse dating to 1874 — one of the most beautiful spaces in Copenhagen. Very peaceful on a weekday afternoon. Budget 1–1.5h.
• The Latin Quarter — The old university neighbourhood surrounding the gardens. Wander along Fiolstræde and Studiestræde — lined with second-hand bookshops, independent cafes, and small boutiques. Very charming, very local. Grab a coffee at Paludan Bog & Café (Fiolstræde 10) — a bookshop café with a lovely atmosphere.

Option B — Nørrebro Vintage Shopping:
• Ravnsborggade & Elmegade — the best vintage and thrift strip in Copenhagen. Hit Episode (Ravnsborggade 11), UFF (Ravnsborggade 9), and Wasteland for quality second-hand finds. Then pop into the indie boutiques along Elmegade. Very local, very her.
• Jægersborggade (10 min walk) — Copenhagen's coolest small street: ceramics studios, zero-waste shops, indie art galleries and The Coffee Collective (no. 10) for the best coffee in the city. Perfect way to end the afternoon.

Option C — Museum afternoon:
• SMK — National Gallery of Denmark (Sølvgade 48–50) — Free permanent collection, impressive building, excellent cafe inside. 10 min walk from Nørreport. 2h is comfortable for the highlights. Send her Option A or B — both are self-explanatory and easy by metro

🎨 Louisiana Museum of Modern Art
Train from Nørreport or Østerport → Humlebæk (~35 min, ~€8 each way, buy on dsb.dk). Louisiana is open until 22:00 Tuesday–Friday — arriving at ~17:00 gives you a full 3–4 hours, which is exactly what this place deserves. The building, the sculpture garden, and the collection are all world-class — and the views across the water to Sweden at dusk are genuinely breathtaking. You'll be tired from Italy but this requires very little effort once you're there.
One of the best museums in Europe — the setting alone is worth it
Entry ~€25pp · louisiana.dk · No advance booking needed

🎓 YOUR CLASSES — Ethical Hacking (10:00–14:00)
She has a solid 4-hour morning/early afternoon window. Suggested itinerary for her:

Option A — Vesterbro: Værnedamsvej + Istedgade:
• Værnedamsvej — Copenhagen's most charming street, nicknamed "Little Paris." A short stretch lined with French-style wine bars, artisan cheese shops, florists, and independent boutiques. Start with coffee at Riccos Kaffebar (Istedgade 119) — one of CPH's oldest and most beloved independent coffee spots.
• Istedgade — Vesterbro's main artery, now a genuinely cool strip of vintage stores, concept shops, and cafés. Den Blå Hest (Istedgade 66) is a great independent boutique. Samarkand (Istedgade 87) for quality second-hand. Completely different crowd and vibe from Nørrebro.

Option B — Museum + design:
• Designmuseum Danmark (Bredgade 68) — World-class design museum in a beautiful 18th-century building. Danish furniture, fashion, and industrial design. €14 entry, ~1.5–2h. Metro: Østerport, then 12 min walk.
• Bredgade antique street — The street the museum sits on is lined with antique and art galleries — great for window shopping after.

Option C — Assistens Cemetery + Jægersborggade:
• Assistens Kirkegård (Kapelvej 4) — One of Copenhagen's most beloved local spots. A vast, park-like cemetery where Hans Christian Andersen and Søren Kierkegaard are buried — locals picnic and sunbathe here. Beautiful tree-lined paths, completely off the tourist radar. Free, open all day.
• Jægersborggade — 10 min walk from the cemetery. Nørrebro's best-kept secret: The Coffee Collective (no. 10), indie ceramics studios, zero-waste shops, and small galleries.

Option D — Designer Quarter Shopping:
• Kronprinsensgade — Copenhagen's boutique street. Acne Studios (no. 11), Stine Goya (Grønnegade 11), Cecilie Bahnsen, Weekday (no. 6). Very different energy from the vintage shopping — this is Scandi minimalism at its finest.
• Strøget — the main shopping street for high street brands if she wants something more accessible. Connects back to Kronprinsensgade easily. Option A (Vesterbro) or D (designer quarter) best if she hasn't done vintage yet — save vintage for a fresh day
14:30
🐠 Den Blå Planet — National Aquarium Denmark
Metro M2 from Nørreport → Kastrup (~15 min), then a 10-min walk. Northern Europe's largest aquarium — 7 million litres of water, 735 species, 48 tanks. Don't miss the Ocean Tank (hammerhead sharks swimming overhead), the Amazon Rainforest zone (sloths, birds, reptiles), the rescued sea otters, and the rays you can hand-feed. The building itself is award-winning architecture by 3XN — shaped like a swirling whirlpool from the outside. Open until 21:00 on weekdays. Budget 2.5–3h. ~€25pp.
One of the best aquariums in Europe — the Ocean Tank alone is jaw-dropping
Buy tickets online at denblaaplanet.dk — cheaper and no queue

🇮🇹 Milano
🏔Lake Como day trip (Apr 4): Take the 07:20 train from Milano Centrale → Varenna (~1h). Buy the free-circulation ferry ticket (~€23pp) at navigazionelaghi.it in advance. Route: Varenna → Bellagio (ferry) → Como (ferry) → train back. April = wisteria season at the villas.
🥐Best pastry: Pasticceria Marchesi (Brera), Bar Luce (Wes Anderson–designed, Fondazione Prada).
🍝Must-eat: Risotto alla Milanese (saffron), Cotoletta Milanese (breaded veal). Don't miss aperitivo hour!
🚇From BGY airport: Orio Shuttle bus (orioaeroporto.it) to Milano Centrale, ~€6, every 30 min, ~55 min.
📱Book Frecciarossa at trenitalia.com or Trainline app. 2nd class (standard) is perfectly fine and much cheaper.
🏛 Roma
🏛Colosseum: Book at coopculture.it — combined ticket with Roman Forum. Go in the morning to avoid afternoon heat and peak crowds.
🍦Best gelato: Gelateria dei Gracchi (Prati — near you!), Giolitti (near Pantheon), Il Gelato di San Crispino (Pantheon). Avoid tourist trap places with towering mounds of colour.
🏠Your location: Ponte Milvio is north Rome. Budget 20–30 min taxi/Uber to the main sites. Villa Borghese gardens are an easy 15 min walk — use them!
🧳April 7 luggage: KiPoint at Milano Centrale (kipointstorage.com), open until midnight. Book online. ~€6/bag.
🇫🇷 Paris
🥐Best pastries: Du Pain et des Idées (boulangerie), Pierre Hermé (macarons), Stohrer (oldest patisserie), Ladurée (classic). All within easy reach of your flat!
🎭Moulin Rouge: Book show-only ticket (not dinner) at 21:00 for best value. Dress smart. moulinrouge.fr
🛍Shopping: Galeries Lafayette (5 min from flat — stunning Art Nouveau dome + food hall), Le Marais vintage/boutiques, Rue de Rivoli (affordable), Porte de Vanves flea market (Sat/Sun mornings).
⛪Notre-Dame: Fully restored and reopened Dec 2024 — the interior is stunning. Free entry, book a timed slot on the Notre-Dame app the morning of your visit. Open Tue–Sat 9am–6pm, Sun 1pm–5:30pm.
🏰Disneyland tips: Buy tickets online (cheaper). Get there for park opening. Download the official Disneyland Paris app for real-time wait times and Premier Access ride booking.
🚇Transport: RER B direct from CDG to Gare du Nord (~45 min, ~€11.80). Buy a carnet of 10 Metro tickets (€17.35) or use contactless card for getting around the city.
